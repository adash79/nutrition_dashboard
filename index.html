Nutrition Deprivation Index — Methodology

The Nutrition Deprivation Score is a composite, weighted index designed to identify the most nutritionally vulnerable Aspirational Blocks. It aggregates 10 nutrition-specific KPIs from the Aspirational Block Programme (ABP) portal into a single score between 0 and 100, where higher scores indicate greater nutritional deprivation. Each KPI is normalised relative to all blocks in the selected state and aggregated using evidence-based weights reflecting the severity and preventive potential of each indicator.

Calculation Process — 5 Steps

1
Collect Raw KPI Values
For each ABP block, the raw percentage value of all 10 KPIs is sourced from the official ABP portal data. Values represent coverage rates or prevalence percentages for each nutrition indicator.
2
Min-Max Normalisation (0–100 scale)
Each KPI is independently normalised across all blocks to produce a 0–100 score, where 0 = best-performing block and 100 = worst-performing block. This removes the effect of different units and scales.
normalised_i = (value_i − min_all) / (max_all − min_all) × 100
3
Direction Inversion (Deprivation Alignment)
KPIs where "higher is better" (e.g. ANC coverage) are inverted so that a score of 100 always means "most deprived" regardless of direction. This ensures all 10 KPIs point in the same deprivation direction.
dep_i = normalised_i [if higher = worse] dep_i = 100 − normalised_i [if lower = worse]
4
Weighted Aggregation
The 10 deprivation sub-scores are combined into a single composite score using a weighted average. Weights (1–4) reflect the severity and preventive potential of each indicator. Total weight = 23.
Score = Σ (dep_i × weight_i) / 23
5
Ranking
All blocks are ranked from highest (most deprived) to lowest score. Rank 1 = most nutrition-deprived block. The top 5 blocks are flagged as priority intervention zones requiring immediate CSR and government focus.
